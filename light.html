Light Incontinence: What Really Helps in Everyday Life?

Occasional dribbling when coughing or sneezing affects many women and men. This form of incontinence is common and usually harmless -- but it can still cause discomfort or uncertainty. We offer discreet, reliable products to help you feel confident and free.

What Is Light Incontinence – and How Does It Manifest?

Light incontinence is often referred to as stress incontinence: urine loss during physical exertion, like laughing, sneezing or exercise. It usually happens irregularly and only in small amounts.

Causes in Women and Men

Common triggers include:

  • In women: pregnancy, childbirth, or a weakened pelvic floor
  • In men: consequences of prostate surgery or weak pelvic muscles
  • For both: age-related muscle weakening

Are There Treatments for Light Incontinence?

Yes – there are many helpful approaches:

  • Pelvic floor training: strengthens the muscles and reduces symptoms
  • Bladder diary & training: helps identify patterns and gain control
  • Diet and lifestyle: managing weight, fluid intake, and avoiding irritants
  • Medical check-up: to exclude or treat underlying issues
